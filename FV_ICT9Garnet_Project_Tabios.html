How to Prevent Indoor Fires

Keep combustible materials away when cooking.		 These combustible materials will ignite if they come in contact with a hot element or gas burner. Cooking is the leading cause of home fires in the U.S. It is also the leading cause of fire injuries. Deaths due to fires caused by cooking are particularly preventable. Never leave cooking unattended.

Use glass or metal fire screen to keep fire in the fireplace.
The screen helps to prevent sparks and embers from flying out of the fireplace, while the glass helps to contain the heat inside the firebox. This combination helps to make your gas fireplace a safer and more efficient heating source.

Keep children, pets and things that can burn away from heating equipment.		 Matches and lighters are tools, not toys. Children set fires for many reasons; they may be curious about fire, crying for help, or engaging in delinquent behavior. Never allow children to play with lighters or matches.

DIFFERENT CAT BREEDS





Persian Cat
  • The Persian cat has a round face and a short muzzle, making it a long-haired breed of cat.

  • Persians have a reputation for being laid-back and friendly, yet they also convey a sense of monarchy.

  • The Persian is an old breed. This longhaired cat originated in Mesopotamia, which was later known as Persia, now modern-day Iran.

Siamese Cat
  • The Siamese cat has a long, elegant body. The body, the neck, the legs, and the tail are all long.

  • The Siamese cat is highly intelligent, yet that does not mean they can be trained to perform all the tasks you wish.

  • The mythical temple cat of the King of Siam is a Siamese. The cats served as guard cats in addition to being treasured by the king.

Ragdoll Cat
  • Ragdoll cats are well-balanced with a medium to large, moderately longhaired, blue-eyed pointed cats.

  • Ragdoll cats have an aura of grace and subdued power, making them have a striking appearance. They are intelligent and devoted.

  • The Ragdoll is a breed whose origins are surrounded by mystery, controversy, and tall tales. We have tales, theories, and fiction instead of facts.

Abyssinian Cat
  • Domestic short-haired cats of the Abyssinian breed are known for their characteristic "ticked" tabby coats.

  • A typical Abyssinian cat is outgoing, lively, and enjoys being the center of attention. They form strong bonds with their family.

  • They are one of the oldest cat breeds in existence. Genetic testing shows that Abyssinian cats are in fact descended from ancient Egyptian cats.
